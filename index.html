Marriage and Divorce in Australia and the World

Marriage is an ancient tradition, woven into cultures worldwide. But not all unions are meant to last,
and divorce is an equally important part of the story. This dashboard present data on Marriage and
Divorce in Australia, and compares these statistics to member countries of the Organisation for
Economic Co-operation and Development (OECD).

A global overview: How Australia compares to various OECD countries

The bump chart showcases how Australian Marriage and Divorce rates
have evolved over time with respect to various OECD countries.
While Australia appears to have improved in comparison to the
other countries, overall marriage rates have been declining worldwide, and
Australia is no exception.

Australia vs the World: Marriage indicators

In this section, let's compare Australia's average marriage
age to some other OECD countries, for both genders.
Australian numbers are fairly close to the global average.
More interestingly perhaps, women tend to get married
at an earlier age than men.

Who's getting married?

Not everyone gets married under the same circumstances.
Sometimes people are getting married for the first time,
and sometimes they've been married before. The stacked
column chart below shows the proportion of people on
their first marriage, people who have been divorced,
and people who've been widowed, by country.

A diverse story

Marriages between different genders has been the norm    
for many years. But times are changing, and same-sex
marriages have become increasingly common in society.
Currently, about 1 in 25 married Australians are
in a same-sex relationship

A global perspective on same-sex mariage

Not all countries are as accepting. Same-sex marriage is
still illegal in many countries worldwide. In fact, it wasn't
until the mid 2010s when same-sex marriage was legalised
in countries that are seen as comparatively progressive.
Same-sex marriage was only legalised in Australia in 2017!
